About

Taylor Swift's eleventh studio album is titled The Tortured Poets Department[a]. Swift, Jack Antonoff, and Aaron Dessner wrote and produced the song, which will be released on April 19, 2024, via Republic Records. Swift made the announcement on February 4, 2024, following her win for Best Pop Vocal Album with her tenth studio album, Midnights (2022). Swift developed The Tortured Poets Department shortly after finishing Midnights and continued to work on it throughout her sixth concert tour, the Eras Tour (2023-2024). The Tortured Poets Department, which Swift characterized as her "lifeline" album, was viewed by her as the pinnacle of her songwriting. It is a double record, with the second half, named The Anthology, being released two hours after the first.

Writing and Lyrics

The Tortured Poets Department is an album that has sixteen standard songs and two guest appearances: American rapper Post Malone on the lead single "Fortnight" and English indie rock band Florence and the Machine, which is led by singer-songwriter Florence Welch, on the song "Florida!!!". Swift wrote and produced the album mostly with longtime collaborators Jack Antonoff and Aaron Dessner; Welch and Malone co-wrote their respective collaborations with Swift.

The Tortured Poets Department, rooted in personal and introspective songwriting, explores the emotions that Swift experienced. Some critics commented that Swift was inspired by her tumultuous relationships to create lyrical narratives that were messy, unbridled, and unguarded,containing meta-references to her personal life through allusions and name-dropping. Heartbreak is the primary emotion, which is expressed via themes of anger, mourning, death, and humor. Swift described the album as "fatalistic" with extensive lyrics about "life or death" that are hyperbolic or dramatic; this is exemplified by the opening track, "Fortnight", whose themes of "longing, pining, lost dreams" recur throughout the album. Besides heartbreak from lost love, other themes include the public perception of her as a celebrity ("Who's Afraid of Little Old Me?" and "Clara Bow") and reinventing oneself ("Florida!!!"). Business Insider's Callie Ahlgrim described the album's content as Swift's "messiest, horniest, and funniest". In The Independent, Helen Brown suggested that Swift's songwriting draws on her country roots to explore detail-heavy narratives.
